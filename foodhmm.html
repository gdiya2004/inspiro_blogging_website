Ambala Chaat House

Golgappa Chaat also known as Pani Puri, is a popular and beloved street food in India, cherished for its burst of flavours and interactive eating experience. Golgappa Chaat is not just about the flavours; it is an experience. The interaction with the street vendor, the anticipation of the bursts of flavours, and the joy of savouring each bite make it a cherished street food adventure. It is also a cultural phenomenon that brings people together, transcending socio-economic barriers, and is enjoyed by indicviduals from all walks of life.

Bengali Sweet Shop

Rasgulla is popular and delightful Indian sweet, originating from the state of West Bengal but cherished and enjoyed across the entire Indian subcontinent. Rasgulla holds cultural significance, particularly in Bengal where it is associated with festivals like Durga Pooja. Dhokla being my one of the favourite treat is popular and savoury steamed cake originating from Indian state of Gujarat. This spongy and fermented dish is widely enjoyed across India and has alot more variations in its preparation.

Mahima Cake House

Cake, a universally loved dessert, comes in various forms, flavours and sizes. My favourite flavour is chocolate filled with choco chips I love it. Cakes are enjoyed worldwide, transcending cultural boundaries, and are symbol of celebration and joy. In essence cake is merely not a dessert but a canvas for creativity and celebration. Whether simple or elaborate, a well-baked cake has power to evoke jpoy and mark special moments in our lives.

Mashoor Jalebi

River Rafting also known as white-water rafting, is an exhilasratying water sport that involves navigating a river's turbulent and fast-flowing waters using an inflatable raft. Participants, often in a group, paddle through the rapids while a guide provides instructions to ensure safety and efficient maneuvering. The experience combines adventure, teamwork, and thrill of tackling various levels of rapids, ranging from calm strecthes to challenging and tumultuous waters. River Rafting destinations are often chosen for their scenic landscape and the varying difficulty levels they offer.

Anand Pizzeria

Naan is popular type of flatbread that originated in South Asia, particularly in regions like India, Pakistan, Afganistan and Iran. Naan is traditionally baked in Tandoor, a clay oven that imparts a distinctive smoky flavour. In essence Naan is not just a bread; it's a versatile and flavourful staple deeply embedded in the culinary traditions of South Asia, offering a delightful complement to a wide range of savoury dishes.

Kachori

Kachori is a popular Indian snack, usually filled with spiced dal, peas or other ingredients, and deep-fried until crispy. It's flavourful and enjoyed across different regions in India. Kachori is a deep fried, flaky pastry filled with variety of savory ingredients. The outer layer is made from wheat flour dough, rolled into small discs, and stuffed with a seasoned mixture. Kachori is served with dal, yogurt, chutney and many more things.

Ice-Cream

Ice cream is a frozen dessert made from a mixture of cream, sugar, and flavorings. The base typically includes milk or cream, sweeteners, and sometimes eggs. The mixture is churned to incorporate air, creating a smooth and creamy texture. Various flavors can be added, such as vanilla, chocolate, fruit, or nuts. After churning, the ice cream is frozen to solidify its consistency. It's a popular treat enjoyed in cones, cups, or alongside other desserts. The diversity of flavors and toppings allows for a wide range of delightful ice cream experiences

Street Food

Street food in India is a diverse and flavorful culinary experience, featuring popular delights such as chaat, vada pav, pav bhaji, samosas, kebabs, dosas, chole bhature, jhalmuri, kathi rolls, and kulfi. These dishes showcase a wide range of spices, textures, and regional variations, making Indian street food a vibrant and integral part of the country's food culture. Momos are a type of dumpling that originated in Tibet but have become widely popular in India. These steamed or fried dumplings are typically filled with a mixture of minced meat (often chicken, pork, or lamb) or vegetables, along with spices. Momos are usually served with a dipping sauce, such as a spicy tomato-based chutney or a tangy tamarind sauce. They have become a beloved street food item across India, with variations in fillings and flavors based on regional preferences
