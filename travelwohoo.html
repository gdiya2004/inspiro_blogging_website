Dal Lake

Dal Lake is nestled in the picturesque Kashmir Valley, is a unique jewel known for its floating gardens, houseboats, and vibrant shikara rides. Its serene waters mirror the stunning Himalayan surroundings, creating a captivating blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. Dal Lake stands out for its floating gardens called "rad" and intricate houseboats, offering a distinctive blend of natural beauty and cultural charm. Shikara rides on the lake provide a unique prespective of the majestic Himalayan backdrop, making Dal Lake an unparalled gem in the heart of kashmir.

Shankracharaya Mandir

Perched on a hilltop in Srinagar, the Shankaracharya Temple, also known as Jyeshteshwara Temple, boasts a unique location that offers panoramic views of the entire city and Dal Lake. the temple's architectural gandeur, coupled with its spioritual significance, makes it a distinctive and tranquil site for devotees and visitors alike. To reachj the temple one needs to climb a flight of approximately 250 steps, offerring a physical and spiritual ascent. The location provides breath takinbg panoramic views of Srinagar, Dal Lake, and the surrounding snow capped mountains, adding to the temple's allure.

Chashme Shahi

Chashme shahi is mughal garden in Srinagar,Kashmir. It was built in 1632, it features terraced levels, water channels, and a central pavilion with a natural spring. The garden's name translates to "Royal Spring". It's known for its scenic beauty and historical significance in the region. Chashme Shahi holds historical significance in relation to Jawahar Lal Nehru, the first Prime Minister of India. Nehru visited this Mughal garden during his time, and it's said that he appreciated its beauty. Nehru always drank water coming out of springs of chashme shahi. water is cold and germs free.

River Rafting

River Rafting also known as white-water rafting, is an exhilasratying water sport that involves navigating a river's turbulent and fast-flowing waters using an inflatable raft. Participants, often in a group, paddle through the rapids while a guide provides instructions to ensure safety and efficient maneuvering. The experience combines adventure, teamwork, and thrill of tackling various levels of rapids, ranging from calm strecthes to challenging and tumultuous waters. River Rafting destinations are often chosen for their scenic landscape and the varying difficulty levels they offer.

Sinthan Top

Sinthan Top is a breathtaking mountain pass located in the Kishtwar district of Jammu and Kashmir, India. Situated at an elevation of approximately 12,450 feet above sea level, Sinthan Top offers mesmerising panoramic views of the surrounding snow clad peaks, lush green meadows, and dense forests. The drive to Sinthan Top is an adventure in itself, with winding roads that provide stunning glimpses of the Himalayan landscape. The pass is accessible by road and is often visited by nature enthusiasts, adventure seekers, and tourists seeking tranquility amidst the scenic beauty of kashmir.

Gulmarg

Gulmarg, often referred as "Meadow of Flowers", is a renowned hill station and skiing destination in the Pir Panjal range of the Union Territory of Jammu and Kashmir, India. The Gulmarg gandola is a major attraction, taking visitors to the Apharwat Peak. It offers breath taking views of Himalayan range. The first phase takes you to Kongdori Station, and the second phase ascends to the Apharwat Peak. The Gulmarg Gondola, one of the highest cable cars globally, provides access to exceptionally skiiing terrain.

Kheer Bhawani

Kheer Bhawani is a revered /hindu temple dedicated to the goddess Kheer Bhawani, situated in the Tula Mula village near Ganderbal in the union territory of Jammu and Kashmir, India. The temple is dedicated to ragnya Devi, aform of Goddess Durga. It is believed color of water changes which is considered as an indication of state of affairs in the region. The central attraction of the temple is a spring that is believed to be sacred.

Tourist Guide

Tourist police in Kashmir, like in many others tourist destination plays a very important role. Tourist police act as a source of information for tourists. They provide assistance regarding local attractions, cultural practices, and safety guidelines. This helps visitors navigate the region more comfortably. Ensuring the safety of tourists is a primary responsibility. Tourists police monitor popular tourist spots, public areas, and transportation hubs to deter criminal activities and provide a sense of security to visitors.
